Show Me The Ballot
US Ballot Complexity and Voter

A Media Party Project • Sept 2024 • Get the code

You are a first-time voter: you registered to vote, checked your registration status, scet time aside to head to the polls, and are ready to exercise your rights as a citizen.

But at the polling station, you are handed a huge piece of rigid paper bearing the names of dozens and dozens people. In some states, you are being asked to vote not just for President and a House representative, but a slew of local judges, state representatives, school board members, county clerks, city council members, commissioners. Instead of empowerment, you feel confusion. This feels a lot like taking a test of filing taxes.

As part of the "AI and Elections" Hackathon held Sept 29 during the Media Party at Columbia University, our team members sought to collect and analyze sample ballots for the 2024 general elections. We collected and processed the content of ballots from the most populous zip codes of each state and had AI chatbots (ChatGPT and Claude) assess the usability and complexity of the ballots.
